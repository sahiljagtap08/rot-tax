# Adversarial Red-Team (pre-data) — full objection log


**Overall verdict:** Not sound enough to run as written. All three reviewers independently reach REJECT-but-resubmittable, and their objections converge tightly, which makes the verdict robust rather than idiosyncratic. The phenomenon is plausible, the pre-registration instinct and the mechanical probes B/C are above-average, and crucially almost every fatal flaw is cheap to fix and must be frozen BEFORE data collection, which is the entire point of pre-registering. There are four structural failures that would invalidate the headline result if data were collected now: (1) position, needle-to-probe distance, and total volume are perfectly collinear, so the central causal claim ('info already in context degrades as context grows') is unidentified and could be a pure lost-in-the-middle re-derivation of Chroma; (2) the 'difficulty held exactly constant' selling point is false for two of three probes and for the realistic-filler arms, re-introducing the SlopCodeBench confound §2 claims to defeat; (3) the validity controls and the LLM judge cannot separate in-context retrieval from parametric recall or agent-rot from judge-rot; and (4) the live-signal validation, the USD rot-tax proxy, and the random-effects/power plan are statistically circular, mis-specified, and underpowered (R=8 gives ~0.35 CI half-widths, the KEY Phase-2 contrast has no power analysis, and the named-but-undefined GO/WEAK/NULL rule plus FDR-on-a-confirmatory-test invite post-hoc rationalization). Separately, the novelty positioning currently locks the pre-registration onto the weakest, most-subsumed claim (rot exists), while the two genuinely citable contributions (incremental live prediction over a T-only baseline, and timing-vs-mechanism isolation via a fork-at-random arm) are buried and not gated by GO. Recommendation: do not collect data until the critical-priority items are frozen into the pre-registration; with the position factorial, synthetic substrate + counterfactual-needle control, T-decoupled probe difficulty, mechanical/pinned scoring, partial-correlation signal validation, a powered and re-specified statistical plan, the fork-at-random arm, and a repositioned headline, this becomes a reproducible and defensible pilot. As-is, an independent rerun on different infrastructure or in a different month would not reproduce the curve or the dollar figure.


## Lens: causal-confound

*REJECT in current form (resubmit-eligible). The fixed-needle/growing-haystack framing is necessary but NOT sufficient to identify within-session "rot" as a context-accumulation effect. The design conflates at least three mutually-confounded mechanisms (absolute serial position of the needle, needle-to-probe DISTANCE, and total token volume) and holds none of them isolated; the "difficulty held constant" claim is true only for the needle's intrinsic content, not for the retrieval/tracking operation the probe actually measures, whose difficulty provably scales with T. The control battery (filler-only-at-chance) rules out the least interesting confound (gross leakage) while leaving the load-bearing ones untouched. Several pre-registered thresholds (|rho|>=0.5, BH "across signals") are mis-specified in ways that will manufacture false positives. The headline causal claim "info ALREADY in context degrades as context grows" cannot be cleanly supported by this design without the additional control conditions below. Most fixes are cheap and must be frozen into the pre-registration BEFORE data collection, which is the entire point of pre-registering.*


- **[blocker]** Position/distance/volume are perfectly collinear in your design, so you cannot attribute any accuracy drop to 'context accumulation' specifically. The needle is at FIXED absolute position (early), the probe is at the END, and you grow filler between them. Therefore as T rises, three things move in lockstep: (1) total token volume rises, (2) needle-to-probe DISTANCE rises, (3) the needle's RELATIVE position (fraction-of-context) moves from ~middle toward the front. 'Lost-in-the-middle' (Liu 2023, which you cite) predicts a drop from exactly mechanism (3) — a U-shaped position curve — that has nothing to do with 'rot' or volume per se. Your own §10 admits position is a confound but waves it off as a 'documented follow-up.' It is not a follow-up; it is the alternative hypothesis that explains your headline result with zero appeal to context accumulation.
  - **Fix:** Add a 2x2 factorial that breaks the collinearity, frozen into the pre-reg as a PRIMARY condition (not a follow-up): cross needle ABSOLUTE position {front, end-anchored} with total volume T. Specifically add a 'needle-at-end' arm (needle placed immediately before the probe, filler placed BEFORE the needle) at every T. If rot is real and volume-driven, accuracy still falls with T when distance is held near-zero. If accuracy is restored by moving the needle adjacent to the probe, your effect is pure distance/position and must be reported as such. Also add at least one mid/late absolute-position condition to recover the U-shape. Without this arm the causal claim is unidentified.


- **[blocker]** 'Task difficulty held EXACTLY constant' is false for Probes B and C, and these are precisely the probes you call mechanical/ground-truthed. Probe B ('list every file edited so far and the most recent change to each') is scored against a harness-injected edit log — but if filler turns contain simulated tool calls and 'rejected diffs' / 'abandoned plans' (your §4.2), then as T grows you inject more edit-like events the model must DISTINGUISH from real edits. The number of distractor edit-events scales with T, so the discrimination difficulty of Probe B rises mechanically with T. That is a difficulty-vs-length confound of exactly the type your §2 claims to have killed. Probe C is worse: an instruction-adherence violation becomes more likely the more tempting tests/-edit material accumulates in filler; you are increasing the BASE RATE of the trap with T, not holding it constant.
  - **Fix:** Freeze the distractor count independent of T. For Probe B: hold the number of edit-log entries AND the number of edit-like distractor events in filler CONSTANT across all T (pad short contexts, cap long ones); only vary non-edit-related filler volume. For Probe C: ensure the count and salience of tests/-edit temptations in filler is identical across T. State the constancy invariant numerically in the pre-reg and assert it in the harness with a hard check. Alternatively, if you cannot decouple them, drop B and C from the difficulty-constant claim and report them only as a separate 'naturalistic-load' analysis with the confound disclosed.


- **[blocker]** Your control battery has exactly one control (filler-only-no-needle must score at chance) and it tests the WRONG confound. Scoring at chance without the needle only rules out gross leakage where filler literally contains the answer. It does NOT rule out: (a) the model answering from PRIOR/PARAMETRIC knowledge of the substrate repo (you propose using a real permissively-licensed Python repo in §4.1 — frontier models have likely memorized popular permissive repos, so Probe A 'what does function X return' can be answered with the needle ignored entirely, and this leakage is constant in T so it deflates the measured drop and masks rot), and (b) the dual case where distracting-composition filler actively suppresses a correct answer the model otherwise knows (attention dilution, not loss of in-context info). Neither is caught by 'filler-only at chance.'
  - **Fix:** Add two more required controls to the pre-reg. (1) NEEDLE-FREE-BUT-PROBED control: ask Probe A with the needle removed but filler present, at every T; this must score at chance AND must not rise with T — if the model answers above chance, it is using parametric knowledge and you must switch to a SYNTHETIC substrate with randomized/obfuscated identifiers and return values (your config already has substrate.kind: synthetic — make it mandatory, not optional, and randomize function names/return values per seed so no parametric shortcut exists). (2) CORRUPTED-NEEDLE control: replace the needle's answer with a counterfactual value; a model using in-context info must report the counterfactual, a model using memory will report the real value. This cleanly separates in-context retrieval from recall and should be a headline validity check, not omitted.


- **[major]** The 'attention-dilution / distractor' alternative explanation is not separable from 'volume-driven rot' under your composition arms as specified. Your three compositions (diverse/redundant/distracting) vary filler CONTENT but every one of them also varies with T, so you cannot tell whether a drop is caused by (i) more tokens (capacity/positional decay) or (ii) more distractors competing for attention (interference). 'distracting' (plausible-but-wrong near-misses) will produce a steeper curve than 'redundant' for reasons that are pure interference, yet your model pass~log(T)+composition treats composition as an additive main effect with NO log(T)xcomposition interaction term — so the design literally cannot estimate whether content modulates the slope, which is the one thing the composition arm exists to test.
  - **Fix:** Add the log(T) x composition INTERACTION term to the primary mixed-effects model and pre-register it as the test for content-dependence. Add a 'volume-matched, distractor-count-fixed' arm: hold the NUMBER of distractors constant while growing neutral filler, vs hold neutral filler constant while growing distractors. The first isolates volume; the second isolates interference. Only with both can you claim the effect is accumulation rather than dilution.


- **[major]** The signal-validation threshold |rho|>=0.5 with BH 'across signals' will manufacture validated detectors by construction and is statistically circular. (a) You correlate each live signal against 'true probe-accuracy drop' where BOTH are monotone functions of T. Any signal that trends with T (output_entropy, self_repetition, file_reread_count all plausibly drift with context length for reasons unrelated to rot) will show |rho|>=0.5 purely through shared dependence on T — a classic third-variable (T) confound. You are validating signals against the very axis you swept. (b) BH 'across signals' (6 tests) is far too narrow a family; your real multiplicity is signals x probe types x compositions x models, easily 100+ comparisons, and you only correct 6. (c) rho is computed across cells that are non-independent (8 seeds share substrate items, milestones share filler prefixes).
  - **Fix:** Validate signals against the RESIDUAL accuracy drop after regressing out T (partial correlation controlling for T), or within-T across the diverse/redundant/distracting compositions where T is fixed but rot differs. Pre-register the family as all signal x probe x composition tests and BH over the full family. Require a signal to predict accuracy drop in a HELD-OUT composition/model it was not validated on (train/test split) before it is eligible for the detector. Add the trivial baseline 'predict drop from T alone' — a validated signal must beat it.


- **[major]** Filler-leakage control is binary and one-shot ('must score at chance') but leakage is graded and composition-specific. The 'distracting' arm is explicitly 'plausible-but-wrong near-misses' to the probe answer — that is, semantically ADJACENT to the answer, which is the opposite of the §4.2 'semantically unrelated' guarantee you rely on to claim difficulty is unchanged. Near-misses can either leak partial signal (a near-miss to 'returns X' narrows the answer space) or actively mislead; either way the distracting arm violates the semantic-unrelatedness premise that the whole difficulty-constancy argument rests on. A single aggregate at-chance check cannot detect per-composition graded leakage because the diverse/redundant arms can sit at chance while the distracting arm leaks.
  - **Fix:** Run the filler-only-no-needle control SEPARATELY per composition AND per T (not aggregate), and pre-register that EACH composition x T cell must be statistically indistinguishable from chance (with a non-inferiority margin, not just 'not significantly above'). Additionally, measure mutual information / embedding-similarity between filler and gold answers per composition and pre-register a maximum-similarity cap; reject any filler batch that exceeds it before the real run.


- **[major]** The pilot proxy RotTax_proxy(T) = input_price x (T - T*) x P(degraded|T) is causally incoherent as a 'tax' and will not survive review even as a labeled proxy. It charges input price for ALL tokens past T* regardless of whether those tokens caused the degradation, and it has no counterfactual: the 'tax' is defined as money spent past a peak, but a naive run that never degrades still 'pays' it under this formula, and a run that degrades early but cheaply pays little. There is no comparison to a governed baseline in the proxy (unlike the real RotTax = cost_naive - cost_governed), so the proxy measures a different estimand than the metric it proxies for — it is not an estimator of the target quantity, it is a separate quantity wearing its name.
  - **Fix:** Either (a) drop the dollar proxy from the pilot entirely and report only accuracy-vs-T curves until Phase 2 produces the genuine cost_naive - cost_governed contrast, or (b) redefine the proxy as an explicit counterfactual on the SAME curve: proxy = input_price x integral over T>T* of [acc(T*) - acc(T)]/marginal_value_per_token, and prove it is a lower/upper bound on the true RotTax. Pre-register which bound it is. Do not ship a 'tax' that a non-rotting run still pays.


- **[major]** No within-context random/temporal-order control: the needle's relationship to the probe is held in a single fixed configuration, so you cannot rule out that the model is using recency/serial-recall heuristics rather than genuine retrieval. Because the needle is always early and the probe always last, you confound 'early information' with 'the specific information type.' A model with a strong primacy bias and a model with a strong recency bias would produce opposite T-curves under your single configuration, and you'd report one as 'the rot curve.'
  - **Fix:** Pre-register at least 3 needle positions per T (e.g., 10%, 50%, 90% depth) as a crossed factor, R seeds each. This doubles as the position-sweep that resolves the blocker above. Report the full position x T surface; the headline claim is licensed only if the volume effect survives at fixed relative position.


- **[minor]** Random-effects structure is under-specified and likely to be confounded: 'random intercept per (seed, substrate-item)' omits a random SLOPE on log(T) and omits filler-batch as a grouping factor. Since the same seeded filler prefix is reused as T grows (you append filler to reach higher T), observations within a substrate-item across T levels share a growing common prefix — they are nested/autocorrelated, not exchangeable. Intercept-only random effects will understate SEs on the log(T) coefficient and inflate significance of the very effect you are testing.
  - **Fix:** Add a random slope for log(T) per substrate-item, and add a grouping/crossed random effect for filler-prefix lineage. Pre-register a likelihood-ratio test for whether the random slope is needed. Verify with a parametric bootstrap over the actual nested structure rather than the i.i.d. binomial assumed in the §7 power calc — which is also why the power number (0.12 drop at power 0.8) is optimistic and should be recomputed under the clustered design.


- **[minor]** Phase 2 fork-at-peak vs blind-restart is confounded by information content, not just timing. Fork-at-peak 'snapshots at detected T*' (keeps the pre-peak context) whereas blind-restart gets a 'short summary.' These differ in BOTH timing AND the amount/fidelity of retained information, so beating blind-restart does not isolate timing — it could be that fork-at-peak simply retains more context. Your own stated KEY claim ('proves timing, not just restarting, adds value') is therefore not identified by this arm pair.
  - **Fix:** Add a 'fork-at-RANDOM-time' arm that uses the IDENTICAL snapshot/retention mechanism as fork-at-peak but triggers at a random (or fixed-schedule) T not aligned to detected peak. fork-at-peak vs fork-at-random isolates TIMING with content-handling held constant; fork-at-peak vs blind-restart then isolates the combined value. Pre-register fork-at-peak > fork-at-random as the actual timing test.


## Lens: statistics-power

*REJECT in current form (major revisions required before data collection). The core scientific question is interesting and the pre-registration instinct is correct, but the statistical plan is underpowered for its hardest claims, the random-effects specification is mis-stated and likely non-identifiable as written, the GO/WEAK/NULL thresholds are not defined as testable quantities tied to power, and multiplicity is hand-waved ("BH-corrected") across a family that is never enumerated. The most damaging gap: the headline causal claim (fork-at-peak vs blind-restart) is a 4-arm comparison whose sample size, effect-size target, and power are never specified, yet it is named the KEY result. With R=8 the per-cell proportion has a 95% CI half-width of roughly 0.35 at p=0.5 — wider than most effects the design hopes to detect. None of these are fatal to the idea; all are fixable before collection, which is exactly what pre-registration is for.*


- **[blocker]** No power analysis exists for the named KEY comparison (fork-at-peak vs blind-restart). The abstract calls this the result that 'proves timing adds value,' yet phase 2 specifies only '4 matched arms' with no R per arm, no target effect size (delta in task success or in priced rot-tax USD), no assumed variance, and no power target. A pre-registered confirmatory test with no powered primary endpoint is not pre-registered in any meaningful sense.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register the phase-2 primary endpoint as a single scalar (e.g., difference in task-success rate, or difference in priced deficit-area) with a minimum effect of interest (MEI) the authors are willing to defend as practically meaningful. Run a power simulation (logistic or bootstrap on the proxy) to fix R per arm for 80% power at that MEI and alpha after multiplicity. State the MEI in USD/percentage points BEFORE collection. If feasible budget cannot reach 80%, either drop the causal claim to exploratory or use a within-session paired design (same seed/task forked at the same point into both interventions) to slash variance.


- **[blocker]** R=8 seeds/cell is severely underpowered for binary probe outcomes and the slope of pass~log(T). At p=0.5, n=8, the cell proportion has SE=0.177 and a 95% Wald CI half-width of ~0.35. After accounting for the random-intercept clustering the authors themselves specify (5 obs per seed-cluster, plausible ICC 0.2-0.4), effective N per probe-x-composition slope falls to roughly 15-22 binary observations. Detecting a moderate logit slope (the whole point) at that effective N, after BH correction across 9 probe-x-composition slopes, will have low and unstated power.
  - **Fix:** Do a pre-registration power simulation. Specify the minimum logit-per-ln(T) slope worth detecting (e.g., a drop from 0.9 to 0.5 pass across 5k->150k is beta ~= -0.65/ln-unit), simulate the full clustered design at candidate R, and pick R for >=80% power after BH. Realistically this pushes R into the 20-40 range per cell, or requires increasing items-per-probe per run (multiple needles per session) so each run contributes more than one Bernoulli draw per probe type. Report the assumed ICC and a sensitivity curve of power vs ICC.


- **[blocker]** The random-effects structure 'random intercept per (seed,item)' is under-specified and likely non-identifiable as written. If 'item' is a needle instance and 'seed' is a run, then each (seed,item) group may contain only 1 observation (one pass/fail), making the random intercept perfectly confounded with residual — you cannot estimate a per-observation random intercept for a Bernoulli outcome. There is also no random slope for log(T), so the model assumes the degradation slope is identical across seeds/items, which is exactly the heterogeneity the 'rot tax' hypothesis is about.
  - **Fix:** Re-specify and crossed-classify explicitly: pass ~ log(T) + probe_type + composition + (log(T) | seed) + (1 | item), with seed and item as crossed random effects, each with multiple observations per group by design (each seed sees all T levels; each item reused across seeds). State the exact crossing and the number of groups and obs/group. If random slopes won't converge at R=8 (they won't), that is itself proof R must increase. Pre-register the convergence-failure fallback (e.g., drop to GLMM with Bayesian weakly-informative priors via brms, or a cluster-bootstrapped GEE) so the analysis is not chosen after seeing the data.


- **[major]** Multiplicity is asserted ('BH multiplicity') but the family of tests is never enumerated, and BH is applied across heterogeneous test types. There are at minimum: 6 live-signal correlations x probe types, ~9 probe-x-composition slopes, the no-needle chance controls, and the phase-2 arm contrasts. Pooling all of these under one BH, or applying BH separately per family without declaring the families, is investigator-degrees-of-freedom. BH also controls FDR, not FWER — inappropriate for a confirmatory primary endpoint where you want strong error control.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register a hierarchy: (1) one primary confirmatory endpoint (the log(T) slope on the primary diverse arm, and the phase-2 fork-vs-restart contrast) tested at FWER alpha=0.05 with a fixed-sequence or Holm procedure; (2) a clearly labeled secondary/exploratory family (per-signal correlations, composition arms) under BH-FDR. Write out the complete enumerated test list with its assigned procedure in the pre-registration table.


- **[major]** The GO/WEAK/NULL decision rule is referenced as 'pre-registered' but no thresholds are given, and they must be defined as functions of estimated effect size AND its CI, not p-values, or the rule is vacuous. A three-way decision based on significance stars is especially dangerous because 'WEAK' becomes an undefined catch-all that absorbs underpowered nulls and lets the authors claim partial success regardless of outcome.
  - **Fix:** Define each bin numerically before collection, ideally via equivalence/inferiority bounds: e.g., GO if the lower 95% CI bound on the log(T) degradation slope exceeds a pre-set MEI AND the fork-vs-restart success delta lower bound > MEI_2; NULL if the upper CI bound is below a pre-set negligible threshold (a TOST-style equivalence test against a null region); WEAK only if the CI spans the MEI (genuinely inconclusive, which should trigger 'collect more data,' not a publishable middle claim). Specify the MEI for the slope and for the USD rot-tax proxy explicitly.


- **[major]** The live-signal validation threshold (|rho|>=0.5, BH-corrected, p<0.05) conflates statistical significance with the validation criterion and ignores the unit-of-correlation problem. With 6 signals correlated against 'true probe drop,' power to reject rho=0 at rho=0.5 is high (~0.95 at n=45 cells), so p<0.05 is nearly automatic and proves little; meanwhile the |rho|>=0.5 bar is asserted without justifying 0.5 as the bar for a usable live trigger, and the correlation's N depends entirely on whether you correlate over runs (n~360, pseudo-replicated) or cells (n~45). Pseudo-replication across the 8 seeds within a cell would massively inflate significance.
  - **Fix:** Specify the unit of analysis for the correlation (use cluster-robust or per-seed correlations aggregated, not pooled raw runs), pre-register it, and replace 'significant + rho>=0.5' with a predictive-utility criterion that matters for the actual use case: e.g., the signal's AUC / precision-recall for detecting the per-run peak, or out-of-sample R^2 from a held-out set of seeds, with a CI. Significance against rho=0 should be dropped as the bar.


- **[major]** The primary 'rot tax' metric is admitted to be a proxy (priced post-peak accuracy-deficit area), but no measurement-error or sensitivity model is pre-registered for it, and pricing introduces analyst-chosen constants (token cost, success definition) that can swing the headline USD number arbitrarily. Integrating an 'area' over noisy per-run accuracy curves with only 8 seeds compounds variance, and the area metric has no stated estimator or CI method.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register the exact estimator for the deficit-area (e.g., trapezoidal integration of pass-rate gap vs a fixed governed baseline), its bootstrap-CI scheme clustered by seed, and a one-line sensitivity analysis over a pre-stated grid of pricing assumptions so the USD claim is reported as a range, not a point. Pre-commit the task-success definition and token-pricing constants.


- **[major]** Bootstrap CIs are specified but the resampling scheme is not, and naive case bootstrap on clustered binary data is wrong. With random intercepts per seed/item and binary outcomes near the boundary (the no-needle control should be at chance, retrieval at high T may approach floor), percentile bootstrap CIs will be biased and can produce degenerate intervals (e.g., [1.0,1.0]) when a cell is all-pass or all-fail — guaranteed to happen at R=8.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register a cluster bootstrap (resample seeds, not observations) and use bias-corrected accelerated (BCa) intervals, or better, report model-based profile-likelihood / Bayesian credible intervals from the GLMM, which behave at boundaries. For proportions specifically, pre-commit to a boundary-robust interval (Wilson or Jeffreys, or Firth-penalized logistic) rather than Wald/percentile.


- **[minor]** The no-needle 'must score at chance' control has no defined pass criterion or power. 'At chance' for a model-judged retrieval probe is not 0.5 and is not specified; with n=8 you cannot distinguish 'at chance' from 'modestly above chance' (CI half-width ~0.35).
  - **Fix:** Define the chance rate per probe type explicitly, and pre-register an equivalence test (TOST) that the no-needle rate is within a small margin of chance, powered adequately — which will require more than 8 control runs. State the margin.


- **[minor]** Scorer validation (kappa>=0.8 on >=50 pairs) reports only a point threshold with no CI. Cohen's kappa on 50 pairs has a wide CI; an observed 0.82 can have a lower bound well below 0.8, and kappa is prevalence-sensitive — if probe-pass prevalence is skewed, high agreement yields deceptively low or unstable kappa.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register the kappa CI requirement (e.g., lower 95% bound >=0.8, not point estimate), increase the labeled set until that bound is achievable, report prevalence-adjusted (PABAK) or Gwet's AC1 alongside kappa, and balance the validation set across pass/fail to stabilize the estimate.


- **[minor]** Composition arms are labeled 'diverse(primary)/redundant/distracting' but the analysis pools composition as a fixed effect across the same R=8, implying the 3-way GO/WEAK/NULL is read off the primary arm only — yet the secondary arms are underpowered to support the moderation claims the framing implies (does distracting filler accelerate rot?). No interaction power is stated.
  - **Fix:** Either drop the non-primary composition arms from the confirmatory plan and label them explicitly exploratory, or power the composition x log(T) interaction directly (interactions need ~4x the N of main effects) and adjust R accordingly.


## Lens: novelty-collision

*REJECT in current framing, with a clear path to WEAK-ACCEPT. The phenomenon is real and the engineering is competent, but as written this is a desk-reject for incrementalism. The central claim 'context-internal info degrades as a session grows, holding difficulty constant' is, to first approximation, the Chroma Context Rot result re-run on agentic-coding filler. The design under-states Chroma's actual coverage (which already includes distractors, haystack structure, and inference-from-related-info, not just exact NIAH), so the stated delta is too thin to survive review. There ARE two genuinely novel, citable contributions buried here: (a) live cheap-signal prediction of latent degradation, and (b) timing-vs-restarting causal isolation (fork-at-peak vs blind-restart). But neither is the headline, neither is de-risked against its specific collision, and the rot-tax cost metric collides with METR half-life without acknowledging it. Fix positioning: pre-register the two novel claims as PRIMARY, demote the probe-vs-length curve to a sanity-check replication, isolate timing from mechanism with a fork-at-random arm, and make the live-signal claim about incremental predictive value over a T-only baseline. Do that and it clears the bar; leave it and reviewer #2 rejects on 'incremental over Chroma 2025.'*


- **[blocker]** The headline claim is a re-skin of Chroma Context Rot, and the design under-states how much Chroma already covers. The pitch is 'Chroma does probe-vs-length, we do it over agentic-coding context.' But Chroma (2025) already ran needle-question semantic-similarity gradients, distractor-interference tests, haystack-structure tests (coherent vs shuffled, where coherent HURT), LongMemEval conversational QA, and repeated-word tasks across 18 models. Your composition arms diverse/redundant/distracting ARE Chroma's distractor + structure axis. Your probe A (retrieval, model-judged vs source) is Chroma's needle-question-similarity setup. The only uncontested new axis is filler = simulated agentic tool calls/observations/dead-ends. That is a domain swap of the haystack, not a new phenomenon.
  - **Fix:** Demote the probe-vs-length curve to an explicitly-labeled replication/manipulation-check (one figure: confirms Chroma-style rot holds for agentic filler). Promote to PRIMARY the two things Chroma did NOT do: (1) prediction of latent degradation from live cheap signals during a running session, and (2) causal isolation of intervention timing. Rewrite the abstract so sentence one is about live-predictability + recoverability, not whether rot exists. Add a 'What Chroma established vs what we add' table listing each of Chroma's five experiments with subsume-vs-extend checkmarks.


- **[blocker]** The single defensible novel claim (cheap signals predict latent probe-drop) is not protected against the confound that signals and probe-drop are both driven by the SAME length variable. You correlate live signals (tool_call_repetition_rate, diff_progress_per_token, file_reread_count, etc.) against true probe-drop with |rho|>=0.5. But probe-drop rises with T and behavioral signals rise with T, so any non-zero rho is guaranteed by shared dependence on context length. A reviewer kills this in one line: rho(signal, probe_drop) is confounded by T; you showed two things both increase with length, which is Chroma plus a behavioral restatement of it.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register partial correlation / nested mixed-model: predict probe-drop from live signals WITH log(T) already in the model as a covariate; signals must show incremental predictive value over T via likelihood-ratio test or delta-AUC, not marginal rho. State the GO threshold on the PARTIAL effect (signals add >=X delta-AUC over a T-only baseline, BH-corrected). Report the T-only baseline as the thing you must beat. Without this the live-signal claim is not citable.


- **[blocker]** The rot tax cost metric collides with METR/Kwa half-life and is not positioned against it. Your proxy = priced post-peak accuracy-deficit area = integral of (governed - naive) accuracy over context, in USD. METR/Kwa (2025) already fit logistic success ~ task-length and define the 50%-horizon as the agent half-life; your accuracy-deficit-area-vs-context is the same success-decay curve with a price multiplier, and the bibliography lists Kwa as bare prior art. A reviewer writes: rot tax = METR decay curve times a dollar constant; monetization is an accounting choice, not a contribution. Worse, pricing an area depends on arbitrary integration bounds and price model, so the headline number is not reproducible across labs.
  - **Fix:** Either (a) drop USD for the pilot and report a dimensionless quantity (post-peak accuracy-deficit area, or excess tokens-to-fixed-success, a clean half-life-style number directly comparable to METR), explicitly citing Kwa and stating you extend success-vs-length to success-vs-CONTEXT-already-consumed holding difficulty fixed, which Kwa does not isolate; or (b) keep dollars but pre-register the exact price model and integration bounds and report sensitivity to both. State in one sentence what rot tax measures that METR half-life provably does not: difficulty is held constant and the x-axis is accumulated session context, not human task duration.


- **[major]** The intervention study's KEY comparison (fork-at-peak vs blind-restart) is the strongest novelty but is confounded by mechanism and under-powered. fork-at-peak conflates (1) TIMING of the cut and (2) snapshotting/forking state vs summarizing. blind-restart+summary discards state and re-summarizes; fork-at-peak preserves a snapshot. A positive result could be 'preserving state beats summarizing' (state-fidelity effect) rather than 'cutting at the detected peak beats cutting blindly' (timing effect). With R=8 seeds/cell and 4 matched arms, the difference-of-differences interaction you need (timing main effect controlling for cut-mechanism) is almost certainly underpowered.
  - **Fix:** Add a fifth arm isolating timing from mechanism: fork-at-RANDOM-point (same snapshot/fork mechanism, cut at a context level NOT chosen by the detector). Then timing effect = fork-at-peak vs fork-at-random (mechanism held constant); mechanism effect = fork-at-random vs blind-restart+summary. Pre-register the timing contrast as the primary intervention hypothesis. Run a power analysis for THAT difference-of-differences and raise R until the minimum detectable effect is justified; show the calc or increase seeds beyond 8.


- **[major]** Collision with Search Discipline (2606.11522), your named sibling, is deeper than 'different topic.' Its thesis: a single aggregate metric can rank the wrong candidate while the disaggregated structure inverts, i.e. a scalar verifier hides multi-dimensional failure in long-horizon agents. Your design leans on aggregate pass-rate (mixed-effects logistic pass ~ ...) and an aggregate rot tax scalar. A hostile reviewer also reviewing the sibling notes your headline number is exactly the reduced scalar that paper warns against, and that probes A/B/C may rot at different rates (retrieval vs state-tracking vs instruction-adherence are different failure dimensions) such that an averaged rot masks an inversion.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register per-probe-type degradation curves and per-probe intervention effects as a REQUIRED disaggregated reporting layer, not a secondary analysis. Add an explicit hypothesis that the three probes have different half-lives (state which you expect to rot first). Cite Search Discipline in methods as the reason you refuse to report only the aggregate. This converts a collision into a citation that strengthens you.


- **[major]** 'Holding task difficulty constant' — the load-bearing phrase separating you from Chroma/METR — is not operationalized and is probably false as the haystack grows. Your filler is simulated tool calls/observations/dead-ends growing 5k to 150k. Longer agentic filler is not difficulty-neutral: more dead-ends and prior observations change reasoning load and distractor density, itself a difficulty manipulation. So difficulty co-varies with T through filler content. Chroma at least used controlled synthetic haystacks; your realistic filler reintroduces the confound you claim to remove.
  - **Fix:** Operationalize difficulty-invariance and test it: the fixed-needle probe is difficulty-constant by construction (same question, same answer at every T), so make that explicit and ensure probe answerability does NOT depend on filler content. Keep the filler-only no-needle-at-chance control AND add a difficulty audit showing the probe's standalone difficulty (answered in isolation at each length's needle) is flat across T. Separate composition arms cleanly so distractor density is a controlled factor not drift; state that any difficulty drift surfaces as a composition-arm main effect.


- **[minor]** Probe C (instruction-adherence, 'never edit tests/') overlaps with existing instruction-drift / Debugging Decay Index work and is the weakest of the three for novelty. A single hard-coded rule is a thin instantiation; reviewers ask why one rule generalizes to 'instruction adherence' as a construct.
  - **Fix:** Either rotate multiple varied injected rules across items so the construct is 'adherence to an arbitrary session-level constraint' (not 'this one rule') and cite Debugging Decay Index / instruction-drift explicitly, or demote C to secondary and keep A (model-judged retrieval) and B (mechanical state-tracking) as the registered primaries.


- **[minor]** The GO/WEAK/NULL rule and validation thresholds (|rho|>=0.5, kappa>=0.8 on >=50 pairs) are stated but not tied to the repositioned primary claims. After repositioning, the GO criterion must gate the PARTIAL predictive value of live signals and the timing contrast, not the existence of rot.
  - **Fix:** Rewrite GO to require BOTH (a) live signals beat the T-only baseline by the pre-set margin AND (b) the timing contrast (fork-at-peak vs fork-at-random) is non-null with CI excluding zero. Make 'rot exists' a necessary manipulation-check gate, not a GO criterion. Keep kappa>=0.8 but report scorer validation per-probe-type since model-judged probe A is the only subjective one.


## Lens: reproducibility-scorer: attacking measurement validity, scorer reliability, drift, seed control, substrate generalization, and whether the headline metric survives a rerun by an independent team on different infrastructure.

*REJECT in current form (major revision before any data collection). The core phenomenon may be real and the design is more careful than most (mechanical probes B/C, pre-registered decision rule, no-needle controls, a named proxy), but three reproducibility blockers must be fixed first: (1) Probe A is LLM-judged with an unpinned judge that is itself subject to the very degradation under study — convert to mechanical/exact-match scoring or fully pin+isolate+re-validate the judge per T-level; (2) the context substrate (token definition, exact filler bytes, needle depth) is not frozen, so 'difficulty held constant' is asserted not controlled, and Liu-2023 position effects are uncontrolled — content-address and release the exact per-cell context and stratify by depth; (3) seeds do not pin live agentic outputs and R=8 is unjustified by power — separate content-seed from sampling-seed, make the agentic substrate replayable/deterministic, and run a pre-registered power simulation for the fork-vs-restart contrast. Additionally, the headline 'rot tax' USD metric is a multiplicative chain of estimated, provider-volatile, peak-detection-dependent quantities and must be decomposed with uncertainty propagated; the live-signal validation must use partial correlations controlling for T or it merely rediscovers that things grow with context; and provider/model drift across the multi-week collection window must be pinned and randomized-against. With these fixes the pilot becomes reproducible and defensible; without them, an independent rerun on different infrastructure or a different month will not reproduce the headline curve or the dollar figure.*


- **[blocker]** Probe A (retrieval) is 'model-judged vs source,' but the judge model is unspecified, unversioned, and likely the same family as the agent under test. The kappa>=0.8 validation is computed against >=50 hand-labeled pairs ONCE, then the judge is trusted across 600+ cells (5 T-levels x 3 comps x 8 seeds x 3 probes x 2 phases). A judge that is itself an LLM running over growing context is subject to the SAME rot you are trying to measure: at T=150k the judge sees the agent's bloated output and may degrade in scoring fidelity. You will not be able to separate 'agent rot' from 'judge rot,' and the headline degradation curve for Probe A becomes uninterpretable. Reruns with a different judge snapshot (providers silently update judge endpoints) will not reproduce the curve.
  - **Fix:** Make Probe A mechanically scorable like B and C: inject needles whose correct retrieval is verifiable by exact-match or regex (e.g., a unique 12-char nonce token, a specific function signature, a numeric constant) so 'retrieval' is graded by string match, not by an LLM. If semantic retrieval is essential, freeze the judge to a pinned model ID + system prompt + temperature=0 + fixed seed, run the judge in a SEPARATE short-context call (feed it only the agent's final answer + source, never the full session), re-validate kappa at EACH T-level (not pooled), and publish judge inter-rater agreement against two independent human raters with the full pair set, not 50.


- **[blocker]** Context levels T are specified in 'tokens' but tokenization is provider- and model-version-specific. A '50k token' haystack under one tokenizer is a different physical context under another, and providers re-tokenize/re-chunk silently. Worse, the filler is 'simulated tool calls/observations/dead-ends from a real repo' — meaning the actual injected content (and thus the position of the fixed needle, the lost-in-the-middle effect, and the true difficulty) varies with whatever the repo produces. There is no statement that the exact token sequence at each T is FROZEN and shared. So 'difficulty held constant' is asserted, not controlled: needle depth (fractional position) drifts as filler is regenerated, and lost-in-the-middle (your own cited Liu 2023) predicts position dominates the effect.
  - **Fix:** Freeze and version the EXACT artifact: serialize the full context string for every (T, composition, seed) into a content-addressed corpus (hash-pinned, released with the paper). Pin needle fractional depth (e.g., always at 50% +/- a controlled jitter that is itself a logged factor) and report results stratified by depth. Define T in characters AND in tokens-under-a-named-tokenizer (e.g., cl100k or the specific model's tokenizer with version), and verify token counts with the provider's count-tokens endpoint at collection time. Run the no-needle control at the IDENTICAL frozen filler bytes.


- **[blocker]** Seed control is underspecified to the point of non-reproducibility. 'R=8 seeds/cell' with 'random intercept per (seed,item)' implies seeds drive both content sampling AND model stochasticity, but: (1) most production coding-agent endpoints do NOT honor a seed deterministically (temperature>0 sampling, batching nondeterminism, MoE routing, server-side caching), so 'seed' does not pin model output; (2) the agent is AGENTIC — it issues tool calls whose results depend on a live repo/filesystem state, so even with a fixed prompt the trajectory diverges; (3) N=8 per cell for a mixed-effects logistic with random intercepts per (seed,item) is badly underpowered for estimating variance components, and you have not reported a power analysis for the KEY fork-at-peak vs blind-restart contrast.
  - **Fix:** Separate 'content seed' (deterministic, controls filler/needle assembly, fully reproducible) from 'sampling seed' (model nondeterminism). For the agent, EITHER set temperature=0 and pin every available determinism knob, OR explicitly treat the agent as stochastic and report run-to-run variance by replaying the SAME frozen context K times and quantifying within-cell output variance before any cross-cell claim. Make the agentic substrate deterministic by mocking/replaying tool results from a frozen transcript (you already simulate filler — extend this so the live repo state is snapshotted and replayed, not re-executed). Run a pre-registered power simulation for the fork-vs-restart contrast and justify R=8 or increase it.


- **[major]** Single-substrate overfit: filler comes from 'a real repo' (singular) and the instruction-adherence rule is hardcoded as 'never edit tests/'. Findings on one repo, one rule, and one agent generalize to nothing. The 'rot tax' could be a property of that repo's structure (e.g., its test layout makes 'never edit tests/' trivially easy or hard), and the degradation curve could be that repo's specific distractor distribution. There is no held-out repo, no second agent model, and no rule-family beyond a single string match.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register at least 3 distinct repos spanning size/test-layout/language, at least 2 agent models from different providers (to dissociate the effect from one provider's context handling), and a FAMILY of adherence rules (e.g., 3 rules of varying salience) with repo treated as a crossed/random factor in the mixed model. Report whether the rot-tax slope is consistent across repos/models or substrate-specific; pre-commit to reporting per-substrate curves, not just the pooled curve.


- **[major]** The headline metric 'rot tax = extra USD a naive run spends to reach same task success as a governed run' is replaced in the pilot by 'priced post-peak accuracy-deficit area,' labeled a proxy. Labeling it a proxy does not make it defensible: (a) 'post-peak' depends on detecting a peak from noisy live signals — peak detection is itself an estimated, seed-dependent quantity, so the integration bounds are random; (b) 'priced' requires a per-token USD figure that is provider-specific and changes monthly (prices dropped repeatedly in 2025-2026), so the dollar number is not reproducible; (c) 'accuracy-deficit area' integrates a curve whose y-axis (probe accuracy) mixes three heterogeneous probes with different baselines. The proxy is a chain of estimated quantities multiplied together with no error propagation.
  - **Fix:** Decompose and pre-register each factor: (1) report the peak-detection procedure as a frozen algorithm with sensitivity analysis over its hyperparameters, and report the accuracy-deficit AREA in accuracy-token units (provider-agnostic) as the primary, with USD as a clearly-dated secondary computed from a published price table with a pinned date and a sensitivity band across +/-50% price. (2) Propagate uncertainty from peak detection INTO the bootstrap CI (resample peaks, not just outcomes). (3) Report per-probe deficit areas separately before any pooling, since the three probes have different chance baselines.


- **[major]** Live-signal validation is circular and vulnerable to multiple-comparisons gaming as specified. Six signals (tool_call_repetition_rate, diff_progress_per_token, file_reread_count, edit_revert_count, output_entropy, self_repetition) are each correlated against 'true probe drop' with |rho|>=0.5 BH-corrected. But (a) several signals are mechanically coupled to T by construction (longer context => more tool calls => higher repetition counts), so they will correlate with probe drop simply because both increase with T — a spurious common-cause correlation, not predictive validity; (b) 'true probe drop' is the very thing being validated, and if Probe A is judge-confounded, the validation inherits that confound; (c) output_entropy and self_repetition are tokenizer-dependent and will not reproduce across providers.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register PARTIAL correlations controlling for T (and for composition), so a signal must predict probe drop BEYOND what T alone predicts. Hold out a validation split: fit the signal->drop mapping on some seeds/items and test peak-detection on disjoint ones. Define every signal in tokenizer-independent terms (e.g., entropy over a fixed external tokenizer, repetition over normalized edit-distance) and pin that definition. Require the signal to beat a trivial 'predict drop from T' baseline before claiming live detectability.


- **[major]** Provider/model drift over the data-collection window is unaddressed. Collecting 600+ cells x 8 seeds across two phases against live endpoints can span weeks; providers push silent model updates, change default sampling, and re-tokenize. There is no pinned model snapshot ID, no collection-date logging per cell, no plan to re-run a stability anchor. Phase 2 (intervention) compares arms that may be collected at different times than Phase 1's degradation curve, so fork-at-peak vs blind-restart could differ because of a mid-study model update, not timing.
  - **Fix:** Pin exact model snapshot IDs (e.g., dated model versions, not aliases like 'latest') and refuse to collect if the endpoint version string changes. Randomize collection ORDER across the full design (interleave T-levels, arms, seeds) so any residual drift is noise across cells rather than confounded with a factor. Re-run a fixed 'anchor cell' daily and abort/flag if anchor accuracy moves beyond a pre-set tolerance. Log collection timestamp, model version, and tokenizer version per row and release them.


- **[minor]** The 'filler-only no-needle must score at chance' control is necessary but not sufficient and its pass criterion is undefined. 'Chance' for Probe B (state-tracking vs an injected edit log) and Probe C (instruction adherence) is not 50% and is not stated; a model can pass C ('never edit tests/') at high rate with NO needle simply because it rarely edits tests by default, so 'chance' for C may be ~90%, making the control nearly vacuous.
  - **Fix:** Define and pre-register the numeric chance/base-rate for EACH probe separately, measured empirically from the no-needle arm, and require the needle condition to exceed the no-needle base rate by a pre-specified margin to count as 'using the info.' For C, add adversarial items where following the rule REQUIRES the in-context info (a task that naively tempts editing tests/), so adherence is non-trivial and the base rate is well below ceiling.


- **[minor]** Bootstrap CIs + mixed-effects model + BH correction are specified but the bootstrap unit and the resampling scheme are not. With nested structure (seed within item within cell), a naive row-level bootstrap underestimates CIs by ignoring clustering, and BH 'multiplicity' has no stated family size (how many hypotheses?). Reruns of the analysis on the same data could yield different CIs depending on undocumented bootstrap choices.
  - **Fix:** Pre-register a cluster/block bootstrap that resamples at the highest cluster level (item, then seed within item), state the exact BH family (enumerate the hypotheses and count), pin the RNG seed and number of bootstrap resamples, and release the analysis script so the GO/WEAK/NULL computation is byte-reproducible from the released per-row data.
