%% ===== abstract =====
\begin{abstract}
Coding agents accumulate context as they work, and a growing body of evidence shows that model quality degrades as that context grows, well before nominal window limits are reached~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle,hsieh2024ruler,modarressi2025nolima,hong2025contextrot}. We call this phenomenon, in the agentic-coding setting, the \emph{rot tax}: the quality a session forfeits as filler-laden context piles up. That agentic context rot \emph{exists at all} is not our claim---Chroma's \emph{Context Rot}~\cite{hong2025contextrot} owns it---and we treat ``rot exists'' strictly as a manipulation check that gates nothing on its own, established here only by confirming the effect transfers to agentic filler under a needle-position $\times$ volume factorial that holds needle--probe distance fixed and so separates volume-driven rot from the \emph{lost-in-the-middle} position effect~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle}.

Our two primary contributions lie elsewhere. First, we ask whether latent rot is \emph{predictable live from cheap signals beyond what context length alone predicts}: a candidate signal must add incremental skill over a $\log(T)$-only baseline on a held-out composition/model split, the ``thermostat'' claim that, if length is all you need, fails honestly. Second, we ask whether intervention \emph{timing} matters \emph{separably from intervention mechanism}: a dedicated fork-at-peak versus fork-at-random arm isolates the value of acting at a detected peak from the value of preserving state, a separation prior static-schedule interventions~\cite{packer2023memgpt,wang2023recursive,jiang2023llmlingua,lewis2020rag} do not make. A secondary contribution reports per-probe-type half-lives---retrieval, state-tracking, and instruction-adherence rot decaying at distinct rates~\cite{adnan2025ddi}---rather than a single aggregate that can hide an inversion~\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}. The phenomenon is positioned as the \emph{temporal} sibling of disaggregated-quality auditing~\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline} and is measured as a provider-agnostic, cost-normalized quantity rather than raw token count~\cite{kwa2025metr,erol2025costofpass}. All hypotheses, conditions, signals, statistical models, and GO/WEAK/NULL decision rules are \textbf{pre-registered}; we report no results in this document. Findings, including publishable nulls, will be reported from a public, hash-pinned harness.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A coding agent that begins a session sharp does not stay sharp. As a session accumulates context---tool outputs, file rereads, diffs, dead ends, and its own prior reasoning---its behavior drifts: it rereads files it already read, re-edits code it already fixed, reintroduces regressions it already resolved, and quietly stops obeying instructions it acknowledged a thousand tokens ago. This degradation is not a window-overflow artifact that appears at the nominal context limit; it sets in well before, and it compounds. Controlled long-context studies have shown that holding task difficulty fixed while growing input length is enough to drag quality down across every frontier model tested \cite{hong2025contextrot}, that the effect is sharply position-dependent rather than uniform \cite{liu2024lostmiddle}, that it survives once lexical shortcuts between query and evidence are removed \cite{modarressi2025nolima}, and that nominal context size badly overstates the length at which a model remains usable \cite{hsieh2024ruler}. In the agentic regime the picture is worse: as agents extend their own prior solutions or evolve a codebase over many turns, quality erodes monotonically and explicit quality guidance lowers the offset without bending the decay rate \cite{orlanski2026slopcodebench, deng2026evoclaw}.

The dominant way the field accounts for this is the wrong currency. Long sessions are reasoned about in cost-per-token: a longer context costs more because there are more tokens to bill, prompt caching makes re-reads cheap-per-token \cite{anthropic2026promptcaching}, and the engineering response is to compress or evict tokens to keep the per-token bill down \cite{jiang2023llmlingua, jiang2024longllmlingua, packer2023memgpt, wang2023recursive}. But per-token price is a misframe twice over. First, listed per-token price is an unreliable proxy for true cost: realized cost is dominated by concurrency and serving conditions, and price-based comparisons routinely invert once those are accounted for \cite{patil2026beyondtoken, chen2026pricereversal, erdil2025inference}. Second, and more fundamentally, cost-per-token holds quality fixed and asks what tokens cost---exactly backwards for an agent whose quality is itself decaying as tokens accumulate. The quantity that matters is not what a token costs but what a token \emph{buys}, and how fast what it buys decays. Cost-of-pass formalizes the dollars behind one correct answer \cite{erol2025costofpass}; we push the same instinct down the length axis of a single run.

We call this decaying quantity the \emph{rot tax}: the excess context an agent must accumulate to hold a fixed level of task quality as a session lengthens, relative to a governed run that resets or forks before it rots. The rot tax is defined primarily as a dimensionless, provider-agnostic quantity---excess accumulated tokens at fixed quality, or equivalently the quality deficit measured in accuracy-token units between a naive long run and a governed counterfactual---so that it is reproducible and directly comparable to horizon-style measurements \cite{kwa2025metr}; a dated, sensitivity-banded dollar figure is a strictly secondary reading, never the headline. The tax is charged only against the governed-versus-naive counterfactual: a run that never rots owes nothing, which is the only coherent way to bill degradation.

The deeper gap is not measurement, it is action. Every result above is a \emph{thermometer}: it reads degradation offline, after the fact, on a static benchmark, across models or across slices. None is a \emph{thermostat}: none watches a single live run, decides \emph{when} that particular run has begun to rot, and acts on it in time. The half-life formalism that describes iterative-debugging decay supplies the math for a fresh-start intervention but is fit and applied offline \cite{adnan2025ddi}. Horizon measurement bounds capability across the population of tasks but says nothing within one run \cite{kwa2025metr}. The interventions themselves---compaction, summarization, paging, retrieval, tool-output compression---already exist, but they fire on static schedules or always-on heuristics rather than on detected rot \cite{packer2023memgpt, wang2023recursive, lewis2020rag, chopra2026headroom, rajasekaran2025contextengineering, yan2025dontbuild}. The structurally closest prior work moves the quality judge \emph{outside} the agent and lets it act at decision boundaries, but along the spatial axis of disaggregated candidate structure \cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}; the temporal sibling---an external detector that watches quality-per-dollar decay over the life of a run and fires an optimally-timed reset---does not yet exist. Closing the thermostat gap requires three things prior work does not jointly supply: that latent rot be \emph{predictable live} from cheap signals, that intervention \emph{timing} be shown to matter independently of intervention \emph{mechanism}, and that the whole apparatus be measured in a currency that does not hold quality fixed.

\paragraph{Contributions.} We pre-register and pursue four claims, sharply tiered by what each is allowed to license:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{C0 (manipulation check, not a contribution).} Agentic-coding context rot exists: accuracy on fixed-difficulty probes falls as session context grows, even with needle-to-probe distance held constant so the drop cannot be explained by position or lost-in-the-middle effects \cite{liu2024lostmiddle}. Chroma established this for generic haystacks \cite{hong2025contextrot}; we only confirm it transfers to agentic filler. It gates nothing on its own.
  \item \textbf{C1 (primary, the spine).} Latent rot is predictable \emph{live} from cheap behavioral signals \emph{beyond what context length alone predicts}. A signal qualifies only if it adds out-of-sample skill over a $\log(T)$-only baseline on a held-out split. This is the thermostat claim; if nothing beats $\log(T)$, we report the null---length is all you need.
  \item \textbf{C2 (primary).} Intervention \emph{timing} matters and is separable from intervention \emph{mechanism}. Forking at the detected rot peak beats forking at a random point with the same fork mechanism held constant, isolating timing from the orthogonal question of whether preserving state beats summarizing it.
  \item \textbf{C3 (secondary).} The probe types have different half-lives: retrieval, state-tracking, and instruction-adherence rot at different rates. We refuse to report only the aggregate, since a scalar can hide an inversion \cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{What is already established, and what is genuinely new.} We are explicit about our debts. That long-context quality degrades under fixed difficulty is Chroma's result, not ours \cite{hong2025contextrot}; that the degradation is position-shaped is \emph{Lost in the Middle} \cite{liu2024lostmiddle}; that nominal context size overstates usable length is RULER and NoLiMa \cite{hsieh2024ruler, modarressi2025nolima}; that agentic quality erodes monotonically over long horizons is SlopCodeBench and EvoClaw \cite{orlanski2026slopcodebench, deng2026evoclaw}; that capability is horizon-bounded is METR \cite{kwa2025metr}; that debugging effectiveness admits a half-life and a computable fresh-start point is the Debugging Decay Index \cite{adnan2025ddi}; that the judge belongs outside the agent is \emph{Search Discipline} \cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}; and that the intervention menu---compaction, memory paging, summarization, prompt compression, retrieval---is rich and available is a decade of context-management work \cite{packer2023memgpt, wang2023recursive, jiang2023llmlingua, jiang2024longllmlingua, lewis2020rag, chopra2026headroom}. Our manipulation check (C0) is deliberately not a contribution; it merely buys the right to proceed. What is new is the synthesis these works leave on the table: a cost-normalized decay quantity (the rot tax) joined to a \emph{live} detector validated against the only honest baseline, context length itself (C1), and a timed trigger designed to isolate whether its value comes from \emph{when} it fires rather than only from the fact that resetting helps (C2). The thermometers are excellent. We are building the thermostat.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

We organize prior work around six themes that the Rot Tax draws on and
extends: empirical evidence that long-context quality degrades; frameworks
that model agentic capability as decay over a horizon; control loops that
locate quality judgment relative to the producing agent; the economics of
agent operation; methods for managing context growth; and the statistical
methodology our evaluation design inherits.

\subsection{Long-context degradation}
\label{sec:rw-degradation}

A growing body of work shows that model quality degrades as context grows,
well before nominal window limits. The needle-in-a-haystack protocol
\cite{kamradt2023niah} introduced the now-standard pressure test of inserting
a fact at varying depths in a long document and probing recall across lengths
and positions; it is methodological tooling rather than a results paper, but
every later study builds on or critiques it. \emph{Lost in the Middle}
\cite{liu2024lostmiddle} gave the first rigorous, quantified result: a
U-shaped position curve in which models use information at the start and end
of context but degrade sharply in the middle, with GPT-3.5-Turbo dropping more
than 20 points and, in the worst case (20--30 documents, answer in the
middle), falling \emph{below} the closed-book baseline of 56.1\%. RULER
\cite{hsieh2024ruler} argued that vanilla NIAH measures only superficial
retrieval and introduced a synthetic benchmark with independently configurable
length and complexity (multi-key/value/query retrieval, multi-hop tracing,
aggregation), finding that although all 17 evaluated models claim
$\geq$32K context, only about half remain satisfactory at 32K. NoLiMa
\cite{modarressi2025nolima} removed the lexical-overlap confound so that
needle and query share minimal surface matches: across 12 models claiming
$\geq$128K context, 10 fell below 50\% of their short-context baseline at 32K,
GPT-4o dropped from 99.3\% to 69.7\% (effective length $\sim$8K), and several
models had effective lengths of only 2K. Most directly on theme, Chroma's
\emph{Context Rot} report \cite{hong2025contextrot} held task complexity fixed
while sweeping input length across 18 frontier models, showing degradation is
universal, non-uniform (sensitive to needle--question similarity, distractors,
and haystack structure), and present even on a trivial repeated-words task.
These works establish \emph{that} and \emph{why} accumulating context taxes
quality; Rot Tax adapts their controlled hold-difficulty/vary-length design
and extends it from raw token count to a cost-normalized decay signal.

\subsection{Agent decay and half-life over a horizon}
\label{sec:rw-decay}

A second line of work models agentic capability as bounded and decaying.
METR's time-horizon study \cite{kwa2025metr} defined the 50\% time
horizon---the human task length at which a model succeeds half the
time---fitting per-model success with a logistic curve and finding the
frontier horizon has doubled roughly every seven months since 2019
(o3 $\approx$ 110 minutes). We note for integrity that METR does not use the
term ``half-life'' or model reliability as exponential decay; that framing
comes from the Debugging Decay Index \cite{adnan2025ddi}, which models
iterative debugging effectiveness as $E(t) = E_0\,e^{-\lambda t}$ with
half-life $t_{1/2} = \ln(2)/\lambda$ and a generalized intervention window
$t_\theta = \ln\!\big(100/(100-\theta)\big)/\lambda$, showing that a strategic
``fresh start'' (clearing history at the computed point) breaks the decay
curve (e.g., deepseek-coder-v2:16b 84.1\% $\to$ 92.1\%). On long-horizon
coding specifically, EvoClaw \cite{deng2026evoclaw} evaluated 12 frontier
models across 4 frameworks on continuous software evolution and found overall
scores collapse from $>$80\% on isolated tasks to at most 38\% in continuous
settings as regressions and technical debt compound. SlopCodeBench
\cite{orlanski2026slopcodebench}, in which agents repeatedly extend their own
prior solutions, showed code quality erodes monotonically (best $\sim$14.8\%
checkpoint solve rate, $\sim$0.5\% by the final checkpoint; erosion and
verbosity rising in the majority of trajectories) and---critically---that
explicit quality guidance reduces the offset but \emph{not} the decay rate.
Rot Tax inherits METR's horizon-bounded premise and the DDI half-life math,
reframing the logistic drop as quality-per-dollar decay over accumulating
context, and treats SlopCodeBench's ``guidance doesn't change the slope''
result as direct motivation for an active reset rather than passive prompting.

\subsection{Control loops and oversight: where the judge sits}
\label{sec:rw-control}

A third theme concerns \emph{where} quality judgment sits relative to the
agent and \emph{when} it can act. In-agent self-correction methods keep the
judge inside the producing model: Reflexion \cite{shinn2023reflexion} reflects
on feedback and stores it in episodic memory across trials (91\% pass@1 on
HumanEval vs.\ 80\% for GPT-4), and Self-Refine \cite{madaan2023selfrefine}
has one LLM generate, critique, and revise iteratively ($\sim$20\% average
gain across seven tasks). Both inherit the agent's blind spots and cannot act
after the agent stops. Orthogonal primitives sharpen the signal:
LLM-as-a-judge \cite{zheng2023judge} supplies a separate evaluator reaching
$>$80\% agreement with humans but carrying self-enhancement bias, and process
supervision \cite{lightman2023verify} places the judging signal at every
intermediate step rather than only the final outcome---the temporal
granularity that makes mid-trajectory intervention possible. The closest
structural sibling is \emph{Search Discipline for Long-Horizon Research Agents}
\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}, which names ``aggregate-verifier
inversion'' (an aggregate metric ranking the wrong candidate first when
validity lives in disaggregated structure) and moves the accept/reopen
decision to an \emph{external} control loop that can demote a candidate the
agent accepted and reopen a run the agent declared finished---two moves a
one-time prompt cannot make. Rot Tax is the \emph{temporal} analogue: where
Search Discipline audits quality across slices, Rot Tax watches
quality-per-dollar decay across time/cost, relocating the corrective decision
out of the agent into a live, external detector.

\subsection{Agent economics}
\label{sec:rw-economics}

Prior work also puts dollars on agent operation. Cost frameworks include
FrugalGPT \cite{chen2023frugalgpt}, which cuts up to 98\% of cost via cascades
at matched accuracy, and Cost-of-Pass \cite{erol2025costofpass}, which
formalizes the expected monetary cost of a correct solution---the canonical
quality-per-dollar metric, observed to roughly halve every few months across
model generations. Inference and serving economics explain why long context is
expensive: cost-per-token/speed Pareto frontiers under hardware limits
\cite{erdil2025inference}, concurrency-dominated real cost spanning
\$0.21--\$15.25 per 1M output tokens on identical H100s
\cite{patil2026beyondtoken}, and the finding that listed price is an
unreliable cost proxy (32\% of comparisons reverse, up to 28$\times$)
\cite{chen2026pricereversal}. The KV-cache lineage---PagedAttention/vLLM
\cite{kwon2023pagedattention}, up to $\sim$24$\times$ throughput---and
commercial prompt caching \cite{anthropic2026promptcaching}, with cache reads
at 0.1$\times$ input price, make re-reading accumulated context
cheap-per-token yet still the dominant cost driver as loops grow. Budget-aware
agents are the nearest neighbors: BAGEN \cite{lin2026bagen} shows agents are
weakly budget-aware (capability$\leftrightarrow$awareness $r = 0.35$) yet
timed early-stopping recovers 28--64\% of tokens on failed trajectories; BATS
\cite{liu2025bats} and BudgetThinker \cite{wen2025budgetthinker} inject live
budget signals to push the cost-performance frontier; BudgetMLAgent
\cite{gandhi2024budgetmlagent} cuts cost 94.2\% via tiered routing; and Token
Economics \cite{chen2026tokeneconomics} frames tokens as a unit of account
with super-linear coordination cost.

\subsection{Context management}
\label{sec:rw-context-mgmt}

A final body of work offers an intervention menu for context growth. MemGPT
\cite{packer2023memgpt} pages memory OS-style; recursive summarization
\cite{wang2023recursive}, Anthropic's context-engineering guidance
\cite{rajasekaran2025contextengineering}, and Cognition's compaction notes
\cite{yan2025dontbuild} compress history; LLMLingua \cite{jiang2023llmlingua}
and LongLLMLingua \cite{jiang2024longllmlingua} compress tokens (up to
20$\times$; +21.4\% with $\sim$4$\times$ fewer tokens, 94\% cost reduction);
RAG \cite{lewis2020rag} keeps knowledge out of context entirely; and Headroom
\cite{chopra2026headroom} compresses tool outputs by 60--95\% in production.
Crucially, all of these apply interventions on static schedules or always-on
heuristics; none frames degradation as a quality-per-dollar tax, measures its
marginal dollar cost, or provides live detection of \emph{when} rot has begun.

\subsection{Evaluation methodology}
\label{sec:rw-methodology}

Our statistical design follows \emph{Adding Error Bars to Evals}
\cite{miller2024errorbars}---clustered and paired-difference standard errors,
variance reduction by resampling, and power analysis---and uses mixed-effects
logistic regression on per-task pass/fail with random intercepts for item, as
exemplified by EWoK \cite{ivanova2024ewok}, so that the context-length
coefficient is estimated net of item difficulty. We report cross-seed variance
per \cite{madaan2024variance} and \cite{hochlehnert2025soberlook}, which show
single-seed small-set evals can swing up to $\sim$15\%, and optionally estimate
per-item difficulty via IRT \cite{maiapolo2024tinybenchmarks}.

\subsection{Positioning}
\label{sec:rw-positioning}

Rot Tax differs from its three closest predecessors along the axes of
\emph{what} is measured, \emph{when}, and \emph{by whom}. Against Chroma's
\emph{Context Rot} \cite{hong2025contextrot}, which offline-benchmarks
raw-accuracy degradation versus input-token count across models, Rot Tax
measures a \emph{cost-normalized} quantity---quality-per-dollar decay over
accumulating agentic-coding context, where relevant facts rarely lexically
match the query, the regime NoLiMa \cite{modarressi2025nolima} shows is far
harsher---rather than token count alone, and does so live rather than as a
static benchmark. Against METR's time-horizon work \cite{kwa2025metr}, which
fits a cross-model logistic of success versus human task \emph{length}
offline, Rot Tax reframes the decay as a quality-per-dollar half-life over
spend/context within a single run and pairs the measurement with an actionable
trigger; the half-life formalism it borrows is from the Debugging Decay Index
\cite{adnan2025ddi}, not METR. Against \emph{Search Discipline}
\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}, whose external control loop audits a
candidate's \emph{disaggregated (spatial)} behavior and acts at accept/reopen
boundaries, Rot Tax is the \emph{temporal} sibling: it watches the same
``the agent is the last to notice its metric has gone wrong'' failure but along
the time/cost axis, detecting \emph{when} rot begins and firing an
optimally-timed reset/compaction rather than auditing slices at decision time.
In short, prior art independently established that quality degrades
\cite{hong2025contextrot,liu2024lostmiddle}, that capability is
horizon-bounded \cite{kwa2025metr}, that decay admits a half-life and
intervention point \cite{adnan2025ddi}, that judgment must be external
\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}, and that interventions exist
\cite{packer2023memgpt,wang2023recursive,jiang2023llmlingua,lewis2020rag,chopra2026headroom};
To our knowledge, Rot Tax is the first to unify these into a single
cost-normalized decay metric with a live detector and a timed trigger that
decides when to invoke one of those known interventions.

\section{Method: A Controlled Rot-Curve Harness}
\label{sec:method}

We measure how agentic-coding performance degrades as session context accumulates, under
conditions strict enough to attribute any degradation to volume rather than to confounds that
prior long-context work has shown can mimic it. The harness assembles every model input
deterministically from a content seed, so that each trial is content-addressed and exactly
reproducible. This section describes the synthetic substrate, the identification design (the
needle-position $\times$ volume factorial), the two-stream filler that enforces difficulty
invariance, the three mechanical probes, and the needle-mode controls. We report \emph{no}
results here; the frozen conditions grid is given in Table~\ref{tab:conditions}, and all
quantitative outcomes appear in Section~\ref{sec:results} \resultplaceholder.

\subsection{Synthetic nonce substrate}
\label{sec:method:substrate}

Each trial is built over a synthetic repository rather than a real codebase. The substrate is a
set of files, each containing functions with mechanically generated names
(\texttt{compute\_$i$\_$j$\_$k$}), and every function is assigned a return value that is a random
12-character \emph{nonce} derived by hashing the content seed together with the function's
coordinates. Because the return values are seed-specific nonces with no presence in any training
corpus, no model can answer a retrieval probe from parametric or memorized knowledge: the only
admissible source of the answer is the in-context needle. This closes the
\emph{parametric-recall} loophole that afflicts naturalistic needle-in-a-haystack
setups~\cite{kamradt2023niah,modarressi2025nolima}, and it makes the retrieval probe gradable by
exact string match rather than by an LLM judge---removing the possibility that a judge model
itself degrades with context and contaminates the measurement~\cite{zheng2023judge}. The
substrate generator is seeded so that the same content seed and substrate item always produce an
identical repository, target function, and authoritative answer.

\subsection{The needle-position $\times$ volume factorial}
\label{sec:method:factorial}

The central identification problem is that growing the filler between an early-placed needle and
a final probe makes three quantities move together: the total context volume $T$, the
needle$\to$probe distance, and the needle's relative position in the context. Under that
collinearity, the \emph{lost-in-the-middle} account~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle}---in which accuracy
depends on \emph{where} information sits, not on \emph{how much} context surrounds it---predicts a
falling curve from position alone, with no appeal to context accumulation. A volume-only design
therefore cannot license any claim that degradation is driven by accumulated context rather than
by position or retrieval distance, a sensitivity that long-context benchmarks have repeatedly
shown to dominate measured scores~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle,hsieh2024ruler,hong2025contextrot}.

We break the collinearity by crossing the needle position with $T$. The needle is the small,
fixed block that carries exactly what a probe needs; the factor \texttt{needle\_position} takes
three levels that place it differently within the assembled transcript:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{front:} \texttt{[needle]\,[filler\ldots]\,[probe]}. The needle$\to$probe distance
    grows with $T$, reproducing the geometry of a naive volume design.
  \item \textbf{mid:} \texttt{[filler/2]\,[needle]\,[filler/2]\,[probe]}, recovering the U-shaped
    position curve.
  \item \textbf{end:} \texttt{[filler\ldots]\,[needle]\,[probe]}. The needle sits adjacent to the
    probe, so the needle$\to$probe distance is held at approximately zero \emph{at every} $T$.
\end{itemize}
The \textbf{end} arm is the decisive one. Because distance is held near zero across all volumes,
any monotone fall in accuracy along the \textbf{end} column cannot be explained by retrieval
distance or by the needle drifting toward the middle; it isolates the effect of accumulated
volume---the quantity we call rot. Conversely, if moving the needle adjacent to the probe restores
accuracy, the effect is pure position/distance and we report it as such. The full
position~$\times$~$T$ surface is reported, but the rot claim is licensed only by the \textbf{end}
column. This factorial is the harness's answer to the identification critique and is what
separates our measurement from a re-run of position-sensitivity studies.

\subsection{Two filler streams and difficulty invariance}
\label{sec:method:filler}

``Difficulty held constant across $T$'' is only credible if nothing about a probe's intrinsic
hardness changes as the context grows. Naively scaling all filler with $T$ violates this:
more filler means more edit-like distractors for a state-tracking probe and more temptations for
an instruction-adherence probe, so a larger $T$ would face a harder question, confounding any
length effect with a difficulty effect. We therefore split filler into two streams with different
scaling behavior:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{\texttt{neutral\_volume}$(T)$:} semantically inert turns---scan logs, linter
    output, generic refactoring deliberation---generated until the context reaches the target
    token budget. This is the \emph{only} stream that scales with $T$; it carries no information
    bearing on any probe's answer.
  \item \textbf{\texttt{structured\_load}:} a \emph{fixed count, independent of} $T$, of the
    difficulty-bearing items: $K_{\mathrm{distract}}$ abandoned/considered edits that act as
    distractors for the state probe, and $K_{\mathrm{tempt}}$ \texttt{tests/}-edit temptations
    for the instruction probe. These counts do not change between the smallest and largest $T$.
\end{itemize}
The harness enforces this with a hard assertion (\texttt{assert\_invariants}) checking that the
structured-load counts are identical at the extreme token budgets, and the authoritative edit log
that the state probe must reproduce is likewise a fixed-size ground truth. Consequently the number
of distractors and temptations a probe must contend with is constant across volume; any residual
hardness change can surface only as a composition main effect, never as a length effect. This is
the design property that lets us interpret a length$\times$accuracy slope as a property of volume
and not of a moving difficulty target, and it directly addresses the concern that aggregate
long-context scores can conflate distinct degradation mechanisms~\cite{hsieh2024ruler,srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}.

The \texttt{composition} factor controls the \emph{character} of the scaling stream while holding
its volume role fixed: \emph{diverse} (primary) uses heterogeneous neutral turns; \emph{redundant}
repeats near-identical scaffolding; and \emph{distracting} interleaves near-miss chatter about
\emph{non-target} functions that, by construction, never states the target nonce. Composition thus
varies the texture of the haystack without altering what the probe must retrieve, track, or
refuse.

\subsection{Three mechanical probes}
\label{sec:method:probes}

All three probes are graded mechanically---no model is asked to judge another model's
output---so the scorer cannot itself rot and results are reproducible across infrastructure. The
probe wording is identical at every injection and does not change with $T$.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{A (retrieval).} The needle is an authoritative note stating that the target
    function returns a specific nonce; the probe asks for that sentinel token. Scoring is
    exact, case-insensitive match of the 12-character nonce. Because the nonce is unguessable, the
    chance accuracy of this probe is effectively zero, which is exactly what a needle-free run
    must produce.
  \item \textbf{B (state-tracking).} The needle is an authoritative edit log---one most-recent
    change per file. The probe asks the agent to list every file in the edit log and its change,
    using only the authoritative log and ignoring the abandoned/considered edits seeded into the
    structured load. Scoring requires full file recall together with the matching change verb,
    computed against the harness-injected ground truth.
  \item \textbf{C (instruction-adherence).} A standing system rule forbids editing anything under
    \texttt{tests/}. The probe is a direct request to fix a failing test by editing the test file.
    A pass means the agent does \emph{not} propose a \texttt{tests/} edit. Scoring is sentence-level
    and mechanical: a sentence proposes a violation only if it references a \texttt{tests/} path
    together with an edit verb (or an affirmative agreement to do so) and contains no
    refusal/negation cue, which separates ``I'll edit \texttt{tests/}\ldots'' from
    ``I won't touch \texttt{tests/}\ldots''.
\end{itemize}
The probes are chosen to exercise three qualitatively different capabilities---locating a fact,
maintaining a running state against distractors, and adhering to a standing
constraint~\cite{shinn2023reflexion}---so that their degradation can be compared rather than
collapsed into a single scalar that could hide an inversion~\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}.

\subsection{Needle-mode controls}
\label{sec:method:controls}

The factor \texttt{needle\_mode} supplies the controls that distinguish genuine in-context
retrieval from artifacts of the construction, and is run per composition~$\times$~$T$ rather than
only in aggregate.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{present:} the authoritative needle is included; the gold answer is the
    nonce it states. This is the experimental condition.
  \item \textbf{absent (control):} the needle is removed but the probe is still asked. Since the
    answer is an unguessable nonce, this run must score at chance (i.e.\ near zero) and---critically
    ---must \emph{not} rise with $T$. A no-needle accuracy that climbs with volume would indicate
    the model is exploiting the filler itself, and flags the batch for rejection.
  \item \textbf{counterfactual (control):} an authoritative note supplies a corrected nonce $N_2$
    after a stale earlier mention of a different nonce $N_1$; the gold answer is $N_2$. A model
    that reads and uses the in-context authoritative information reports $N_2$, whereas one falling
    back on recency or other heuristics reports $N_1$. This separates true context use from
    surface heuristics that an exact-match retrieval probe alone could not
    distinguish~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle,modarressi2025nolima}.
\end{itemize}

\section{Analysis Plan and Pre-Registration}
\label{sec:analysis}

This section pre-registers, in full, the analyses that license each
contribution \emph{before} any data are collected. It is frozen: the model
specifications, the power target, the multiplicity hierarchy, and the
numerical decision rule below are fixed in advance, and any departure is
logged as a deviation rather than absorbed silently. We report \emph{no}
results here; throughout, quantities that will be estimated from data are
written as placeholders (e.g.\ \textsc{[result]}) and the pre-set thresholds
against which they will be judged are stated explicitly. The motivation is
standard for evaluation work where analytic flexibility can manufacture
apparent findings~\cite{hochlehnert2025soberlook}; pre-committing the
estimands, the error bars, and the stopping rule is what makes a null
publishable rather than a failure.

\subsection{Estimands and units of analysis}
\label{sec:estimands}

The atomic observation is a single probe outcome, a Bernoulli pass/fail
recorded per cell of the frozen grid (context length $T$, needle position,
probe type, composition, needle mode, content-seed, substrate, model). Cell
accuracy $\mathrm{acc}(T,\mathrm{pos},\mathrm{probe},\mathrm{comp})$ is the
mean pass-rate within a cell. Because outcomes within a content-seed share an
assembly and are not independent, the \emph{unit of analysis is the
seed-level cluster}, never the pooled raw run: every confidence interval is
seed-clustered or seed-bootstrapped to avoid pseudo-replication, in line with
recent guidance on putting honest error bars on language-model
evaluations~\cite{miller2024errorbars} and on the non-trivial variance of
benchmark estimates~\cite{madaan2024variance}. Interval estimates for cell
accuracies use a seed-level nonparametric bootstrap.

\subsection{C1: live-signal validation as incremental skill over a
length-only baseline}
\label{sec:c1}

The primary contribution C1 asserts that latent rot is predictable
\emph{live} from cheap process signals --- tool-call repetition rate,
diff-progress per token, file-reread count, edit-revert count, output
entropy, and self-repetition --- \emph{beyond what context length alone
predicts}. The identification hazard is that both the candidate signals and
the probe-failure rate rise with $T$, so any raw signal--failure correlation
is confounded by length. The whole of C1 is therefore phrased as
\emph{incremental} skill over a $\log(T)$-only baseline; the contribution is
not ``a signal correlates with failure'' but ``a signal carries information a
length meter does not already give you for free.''

\paragraph{Screening: partial association controlling for $\log(T)$.}
As a pre-registered screen, for each signal we compute a partial
rank association between the signal and probe failure with $\log(T)$ partialled
out of both. Concretely, failure $y$ and the signal $x$ are each
rank-transformed and linearly residualized on the rank of $\log(T)$, and the
Pearson correlation of the residuals is the partial Spearman statistic. The
raw (length-confounded) correlation is reported alongside, but only to
exhibit the confound; the partial association is the screening quantity. This
mirrors the harness implementation, which residualizes on $\log(T)$ before
testing. Screening operates in the exploratory tier
(Section~\ref{sec:multiplicity}) under Benjamini--Hochberg control across the
enumerated signal $\times$ probe $\times$ composition ($\times$ model) family,
and a signal must additionally clear a non-trivial partial-association
magnitude to pass the screen. Passing the screen is necessary but not
sufficient: it does not by itself license C1.

\paragraph{Confirmatory: held-out incremental $\Delta$AUC.}
The registered confirmatory test for C1 is predictive, not in-sample. We fit
two nested classifiers of probe failure: a baseline
$\texttt{fail}\sim\log(T)$ and an augmented
$\texttt{fail}\sim\log(T)+\texttt{signal}$. Eligibility requires (i) that the
augmented model add skill over the baseline by a likelihood-ratio test
\emph{and} (ii) that it improve \emph{out-of-sample} discrimination, measured
as the area under the ROC curve, on a held-out split. Crucially the split is
along \emph{composition and model}, not a random row split: a signal that
only predicts failure within the regime it was tuned on does not generalize
and does not count. The confirmatory effect size is the held-out
$\Delta\mathrm{AUC}$ of the augmented model relative to the $\log(T)$-only
baseline, with a seed-clustered bootstrap interval. The pre-registered
non-inferiority margin is $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}\geq 0.03$: a signal must beat
the length-only meter by at least this much on unseen compositions and models
to be declared informative. If no signal clears the margin, C1 is declared a
null --- ``length is all you need'' --- and that null is reported as such.
Tying the claim to held-out predictive lift, rather than to in-sample
significance, is what makes the ``live thermostat'' reading defensible and
guards against reading rot into a meter that merely re-derives context
length.

\subsection{Re-specified primary model: crossed random effects with random
slope and a $\log(T)\times$composition interaction}
\label{sec:primary-model}

The degradation surface is analyzed with a crossed-random-effects logistic
mixed model, re-specified from the pilot's earlier fixed-effects-only form to
make the heterogeneity the hypothesis is actually about estimable:
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
\texttt{fail} \;\sim\;& \log(T) + \texttt{needle\_position} +
\texttt{probe\_type} + \texttt{composition} \\
&{}+ \log(T)\!:\!\texttt{composition}
+ \big(\log(T)\,\big|\,\texttt{content\_seed}\big)
+ \big(1\,\big|\,\texttt{substrate\_item}\big).
\end{split}
\label{eq:primary}
\end{equation}
Three features are deliberate. First, the model is \emph{crossed}: a random
intercept for \texttt{content\_seed} and a separate random intercept for
\texttt{substrate\_item} acknowledge that seeds and substrates are independent
sources of variance rather than nested replicates. Second, the random
\emph{slope} on $\log(T)$ per content-seed lets the degradation \emph{rate}
vary across seeds; since the hypothesis is precisely that rot rate is
heterogeneous and partly predictable, a model with only random intercepts
would assume away the effect of interest and understate uncertainty in the
slope. Third, the fixed $\log(T)\!:\!\texttt{composition}$ interaction encodes
the registered expectation that composition (diverse, redundant, distracting)
modulates the slope of degradation, so the composition effect is allowed to
act on the rate, not merely on the level. Position and probe type enter as
fixed effects so the decisive identification contrast --- accuracy versus $T$
at \texttt{needle\_position}\,$=$\,\texttt{end}, where needle-to-probe
distance is held near zero --- is recoverable within the same fit and is not
contaminated by the lost-in-the-middle position
curve~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle}.

\paragraph{Pre-committed fallbacks.}
Random-slope crossed GLMMs can fail to converge or yield singular fits. We
pre-commit, in fixed order, two fallbacks so the choice is not made after
seeing the estimates: (i) a cluster-bootstrapped generalized estimating
equation with seed as the cluster, and (ii) a weakly-informative Bayesian
GLMM with the same fixed and random structure. The fallback is triggered only
by a documented convergence failure of Eq.~\eqref{eq:primary}, and which
fallback was used is logged.

\subsection{Power simulation and the rejection of $R=8$}
\label{sec:power}

The number of content-seeds per cell, $R$, is not assumed; it is set by a
pre-registration power simulation (\texttt{harness/power\_sim.py}) so that the
two confirmatory endpoints --- the C1 held-out $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}$ test and
the C2 timing contrast --- reach at least $80\%$ power at their stated minimum
effects of interest \emph{after} the multiplicity correction of
Section~\ref{sec:multiplicity}. The simulation uses a deliberately
conservative, defensible proxy for the primary degradation contrast: a
two-proportion comparison of peak-versus-largest-$T$ accuracy whose variance
is inflated by a clustering design effect
$\mathrm{deff}=1+(m-1)\cdot\mathrm{ICC}$, with $m$ observations per seed-cluster
and a plausible intraclass-correlation range. Because the proxy ignores the
extra information the full random-slope GLMM extracts, its power is a
\emph{lower bound} on the achievable power; an $R$ that suffices under the
proxy suffices under the registered model. The simulation sweeps $R$ across a
grid for strong, moderate, and subtle degradation scenarios at each ICC, and
the registered rule is to pick the smallest $R$ that delivers $\geq 0.80$
power in the assumed scenario and ICC after multiplicity. The two endpoints
are sized separately, and the C2 timing contrast typically demands the
\emph{largest} $R$; to hold its variance down we use a within-session paired
fork (the same seed forked into the fork-at-random and fork-at-peak arms) so
the timing comparison is paired rather than between-subjects.

The pilot's placeholder $R=8$ is \emph{explicitly rejected} by this
simulation. At $R=8$ the seed-level estimate is too noisy --- a confidence
half-width on the order of the effects we care about --- and the proxy power
falls below $0.80$ in every scenario except the implausibly strong-rot case.
Reporting at $R=8$ would risk a high-variance, irreproducible headline of
exactly the kind that motivates pre-registration in the first
place~\cite{hochlehnert2025soberlook,madaan2024variance}; the final $R$ is
whatever the simulation returns, and $R=8$ is retained only as the rejected
baseline against which the chosen $R$ is justified.

\subsection{Multiplicity hierarchy}
\label{sec:multiplicity}

The full battery of tests is enumerated in advance and partitioned into two
tiers with different error-control regimes, so that the small number of
load-bearing claims are protected against family-wise error while the wide
exploratory surface is controlled for false-discovery rate.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Confirmatory tier (family-wise error rate, $\alpha=0.05$).}
  This tier contains exactly two tests: the C1 incremental-skill test (the
  held-out $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}$ over the $\log(T)$-only baseline of
  Section~\ref{sec:c1}) and the C2 timing contrast (fork-at-peak versus
  fork-at-random, mechanism held constant). FWER across this two-member family
  is controlled by a Holm / fixed-sequence procedure at $\alpha=0.05$. These
  are the claims the paper stands or falls on, and they get the strict
  correction.
  \item \textbf{Exploratory tier (Benjamini--Hochberg false-discovery
  rate).} This tier contains the per-signal $\times$ probe $\times$
  composition ($\times$ model) partial associations used as the C1 screen, the
  composition-arm comparisons, and the per-probe half-lives (C3). BH-FDR is
  applied across the full enumerated exploratory family. Findings here are
  flagged as exploratory and are never promoted into confirmatory claims
  post hoc.
\end{itemize}

Refusing to collapse the per-probe analyses (retrieval, state-tracking,
instruction-adherence) into a single aggregate is deliberate: an aggregate
scalar can hide an inversion across probe types, a failure mode documented for
long-horizon agent evaluation~\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}, so C3
half-lives are reported per probe within the exploratory tier rather than
summarized away.

\subsection{Numerical decision rule: \textsc{go} / \textsc{weak} /
\textsc{null}}
\label{sec:decision}

The pilot terminates in one of three pre-registered verdicts, decided purely
by interval bounds and equivalence testing --- no significance stars, no
post-hoc narrative. Let the C1 effect be the held-out $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}$
with margin $\delta_{1}=0.03$ (Section~\ref{sec:c1}), let the C2 effect be the
fork-at-peak-minus-fork-at-random timing contrast with its pre-set minimum
effect of interest $\mathrm{MEI}_{2}$, and let all bounds be the limits of
$95\%$ seed-clustered intervals.

\begin{description}
  \item[\textsc{go}.] Declared iff \emph{all three} hold: the lower $95\%$
  bound of the C1 held-out $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}$ exceeds $\delta_{1}$;
  \emph{and} the lower bound of the C2 timing contrast exceeds
  $\mathrm{MEI}_{2}$; \emph{and} the C0 manipulation check is positive at
  \texttt{needle\_position}\,$=$\,\texttt{end} (i.e.\ accuracy still falls with
  $T$ at near-zero needle-to-probe distance, isolating volume-driven rot from
  position). The placeholder values are \textsc{[c1\_dauc\_lo]},
  \textsc{[c2\_timing\_lo]}, and \textsc{[c0\_end\_verdict]}.
  \item[\textsc{null}.] Declared iff the effects are statistically equivalent
  to negligible: a two one-sided tests (TOST) equivalence procedure rejects
  the presence of a meaningful effect, i.e.\ the upper $95\%$ bound lies
  inside the pre-set negligible region. A \textsc{null} is a publishable
  outcome --- ``length is all you need'' for C1, or ``timing does not separate
  from mechanism'' for C2 --- and is reported as a finding, not a failed
  experiment.
  \item[\textsc{weak}.] Declared otherwise, i.e.\ when an interval spans its
  MEI so the data neither confirm the effect nor establish equivalence to
  null. A \textsc{weak} verdict is explicitly \emph{inconclusive}: the
  registered response is to collect more data, never to report an
  intermediate effect as if it were a positive finding.
\end{description}

Pairing a lower-bound criterion for \textsc{go} with a TOST equivalence
criterion for \textsc{null} makes the two informative verdicts symmetric and
forces the inconclusive region to be named rather than silently rounded toward
the more interesting claim. The controls of the design feed this rule as gates
rather than as headline numbers: the no-needle control must sit at chance and
must not rise with $T$, and the per-probe chance baselines are tested for
non-inferiority by TOST rather than by a bare ``not significant,'' so that a
control passing is an affirmative equivalence statement and not merely an
absence of evidence.

\section{Intervention Study: Isolating Timing from Mechanism}
\label{sec:intervention}

The intervention study (Phase~2) tests C2: whether acting at the \emph{detected}
peak beats acting blindly, and whether that value is attributable to \emph{timing}
rather than to the intervention mechanism. We run five matched arms on the same
tasks: (1) a \emph{naive} long session run to completion; (2) \emph{blind periodic
compaction} that summarizes on a fixed token schedule; (3) \emph{blind restart}
that starts a fresh session with a short carried summary on a fixed schedule;
(4) \emph{fork-at-random-time}, which uses the identical snapshot-and-fork
mechanism as arm~5 but cuts at a context level \emph{not} chosen by the detector;
and (5) \emph{fork-at-peak}, which snapshots the session at the detector's peak and
branches from there.

The contrasts are deliberately decomposed. The \emph{timing} effect---our primary
C2 hypothesis---is arm~5 versus arm~4, with the fork-and-snapshot mechanism held
constant; this is the only comparison that isolates the value of acting \emph{when}
the detector fires from the generic value of resetting at all. The \emph{mechanism}
effect is arm~4 versus arm~3, separating state-preserving forks from
summary-based restarts. Conflating these (e.g.\ comparing fork-at-peak directly to
blind restart) would confound timing with state fidelity, so we report both
contrasts separately. To cut variance on the primary contrast we use within-session
\emph{paired forking}: the same seed and pre-peak trajectory are forked into both
arm~4 and arm~5, yielding a paired difference rather than a between-cell one. Power
for this contrast is sized by the same simulation that sizes the main grid
(Section~\ref{sec:analysis}).

The endpoint is the dimensionless rot tax recovered---the reduction in excess
context-to-fixed-quality (equivalently, recovered accuracy-token deficit) of a
governed arm relative to naive---with a dated, sensitivity-banded dollar figure as a
strictly secondary reading. We pre-register that a null result here (timing does not
beat random forking) is a publishable finding: it would show that for the models and
tasks studied, \emph{when} to reset matters less than \emph{whether} to, which is
itself decision-relevant for anyone building a governor.

\section{Limitations, Reproducibility, and Ethics}
\label{sec:limitations}

\subsection{Threats to Validity}
\label{sec:threats}

We organize threats to validity around the identification, measurement, and
generalization risks that this pre-registered design is built to neutralize, and
we are explicit about those it cannot.

\paragraph{Construct and identification threats (internal validity).}
The central internal-validity threat is that an apparent decay with growing
session context $T$ is in fact a \emph{position} or \emph{needle$\rightarrow$probe
distance} effect rather than a volume effect---the \emph{lost-in-the-middle}
account~\cite{liu2024lostmiddle}, which predicts a curve from needle position
alone with no appeal to context accumulation. We address this with the
needle-position $\times$ volume factorial (\S\ref{sec:method:factorial}): the rot
claim is licensed only by the \texttt{end} arm, in which the needle is adjacent
to the probe and the needle$\rightarrow$probe distance is held at approximately
zero across every $T$. A second identification threat is \emph{difficulty drift}:
if the intrinsic hardness of a probe co-varies with $T$, any length effect is
confounded with a composition effect, a failure mode documented for long-horizon
iterative coding agents~\cite{orlanski2026slopcodebench}. We separate the filler
into a \texttt{neutral\_volume}$(T)$ stream that scales with $T$ and a
\texttt{structured\_load} of fixed count independent of $T$, assert these counts
in code, and run a standalone difficulty audit. A third threat is
\emph{construction artifact}: the model may exploit the filler itself. We guard
this with a per-composition$\times T$ no-needle control evaluated under a TOST
non-inferiority test against an empirically defined per-probe chance baseline, and
with a filler$\leftrightarrow$gold similarity cap. We refuse to report only the
aggregate across probe types, since a scalar can hide an inversion between probe
families~\cite{srinivasan2026searchdiscipline}; the retrieval, state-tracking, and
instruction-adherence probes are reported separately
(cf.~\cite{hsieh2024ruler,modarressi2025nolima}).

\paragraph{Measurement threats.}
Parametric or memorized recall would let a model answer without using the
in-context needle. The substrate is therefore synthetic, with function names and
return values drawn as random nonces per content-seed, so no answer is available
from pretraining knowledge~\cite{kamradt2023niah}. The retrieval probe is
mechanical---a unique 12-character nonce scored by exact string match---which
removes the ``the judge rots too'' confound inherent to LLM-graded
scoring~\cite{zheng2023judge}. A counterfactual/corrupted-needle control
distinguishes a model that uses in-context information from one exploiting recency
or other heuristics. Eval-level measurement noise is a known source of spurious
findings~\cite{miller2024errorbars,madaan2024variance}, which motivates the
clustered, CI-based reporting described below.

\paragraph{Tokenizer and provider drift.}
Because $T$ is defined in tokens, an undocumented change to the tokenizer or to the
served model would silently move the independent variable. We pin dated model IDs,
record $T$ in both characters and tokens under a \emph{named} tokenizer verified
against the provider count-tokens endpoint at collection, and log model version,
tokenizer version, and timestamp per row. A \emph{drift-anchor} cell is re-run
periodically during collection, and the run aborts if anchor accuracy moves beyond
a pre-set tolerance. These measures bound, but cannot wholly eliminate, drift in a
hosted, non-open substrate; we treat this as a residual limitation rather than a
solved problem.

\paragraph{Statistical-conclusion threats.}
Pooling raw runs would induce pseudo-replication. The unit of analysis is
per-seed/cluster-robust, never pooled raw runs, and the primary model carries
crossed random effects with a random slope on $\log(T)$
(\S\ref{sec:primary-model}). Reproducibility pitfalls in reasoning-model
evaluation~\cite{hochlehnert2025soberlook} and the variance structure of small
eval sets~\cite{miller2024errorbars,maiapolo2024tinybenchmarks} inform our
decision rule, which is stated numerically via CI bounds and TOST equivalence
rather than significance stars, and our refusal to report a publishable middle
claim when a confidence interval spans the minimum effect of interest.

\paragraph{External-validity threats and the lab/field separation.}
The controlled study runs on a synthetic substrate, which maximizes internal
validity at the cost of ecological realism. We therefore draw a hard line between
the lab and the field: the controlled lab establishes causality, whereas breadth
must be established separately by the opt-in field study (\S\ref{sec:ethics}). The
two are \emph{reported separately and never blended}; no claim about live coding
sessions is made from the controlled pilot. The chief remaining external-validity
risks are \emph{single-substrate overfit} and \emph{single-provider overfit}: a
result that holds on one synthetic repository or one model family may not
transfer. The design requires at least three synthetic substrates for the pilot
plus a real-repo robustness substrate, and at least two pinned dated model IDs
drawn from at least two providers, so that any reported effect must survive
substrate and provider variation rather than resting on a single point. Even so,
two providers do not span the space of deployed systems, and we frame the pilot
accordingly. The metric itself is a potential threat---an arbitrary cost unit
could drive the headline---so the primary rot-tax quantity is dimensionless
(excess context-to-fixed-quality, or the accuracy-token deficit area), with the
monetary version held strictly secondary
(\S\ref{sec:reproducibility})~\cite{erol2025costofpass}.

\subsection{Reproducibility}
\label{sec:reproducibility}

The design is a frozen pre-registration: the conditions, operational definitions,
statistical model, multiplicity hierarchy, and GO/WEAK/NULL decision rule are
fixed before any data collection, and every subsequent change is recorded in an
append-only deviation log for provenance.

\paragraph{Hash-pinned context artifacts.}
Every cell's full context string is deterministically serialized and
content-addressed (hash-pinned), and the artifacts are released. This makes the
exact input to every model call recoverable and independently verifiable, rather
than regenerated from a possibly-changed assembly procedure.

\paragraph{Named tokenizer and dated model IDs.}
$T$ is reported in characters and in tokens under a named tokenizer, verified at
collection against the provider count-tokens endpoint, and each agent model is a
pinned, dated model ID. Per row we log the model version, tokenizer version, and
timestamp, so that a future replication can detect any silent change to the
substrate.

\paragraph{Content-seed versus sampling-seed.}
We separate the \emph{content-seed}, which deterministically governs context
assembly (including the per-seed random nonces), from the \emph{sampling-seed},
which governs model stochasticity. Generation is run at temperature~$0$ with all
available determinism knobs pinned; where the endpoint remains non-deterministic,
each frozen context is replayed $K$ times and within-cell variance is reported
rather than concealed. This two-seed discipline lets a replicator reproduce the
exact inputs even when the provider cannot guarantee bit-exact outputs.

\paragraph{Randomized collection order and drift anchor.}
Collection order is randomized and interleaved (via a \texttt{--shuffle} control)
so that slow drift in the hosted endpoint cannot align with any factor in the
grid. The drift-anchor cell is re-run periodically and aborts the run on
out-of-tolerance movement, time-stamping the boundary of any data affected by a
mid-collection change.

\paragraph{Released code and pre-committed analysis.}
The harness implements the invariant assertions, the controls, the power
simulation that fixes the sample size $R$, and the pre-specified primary model
with its pre-committed convergence fallback, so that the analysis path is fixed
in advance rather than chosen after seeing the data. Numeric results,
sample sizes, effect estimates, and confidence intervals are deliberately omitted
from this version; the assembler inserts explicit placeholders where reported
quantities will appear once data exist. Where a monetary figure is eventually
reported, it is computed as the dimensionless quantity multiplied by a pinned,
dated price table and is accompanied by a $\pm 50\%$ price-sensitivity band, so
that the headline does not depend on a single volatile price
point~\cite{erol2025costofpass}.

\subsection{Ethics}
\label{sec:ethics}

\paragraph{Scope of the field study and the consent boundary.}
The breadth claim depends on an opt-in field study of real coding sessions, and
its central ethical commitment is a strict consent and minimization boundary. The
field study is \emph{opt-in} and \emph{metadata-only}: it collects derived
session signals and outcome metadata, and it does \emph{not} collect source code
or prompts. This boundary is a hard design constraint, not a configurable option,
and it is the reason the field study is reported separately from the controlled
lab. Because no source code or prompt text is ingested, the field study cannot
expose proprietary code, secrets, or user-authored content; the live signals used
for the predictability analysis are defined in tokenizer-independent terms over
session-level events rather than over content.

\paragraph{Separation of causal and breadth claims.}
We do not make any claim about live coding sessions until the opt-in,
metadata-only field study runs, and we never blend its breadth evidence with the
lab's causal evidence. ``Rot exists'' is treated as a manipulation check, never as
a headline, which guards against overstating the practical reach of a controlled,
synthetic-substrate result.

\paragraph{Research integrity.}
The frozen pre-registration, the enumerated multiplicity hierarchy, the
numeric and TOST-based GO/WEAK/NULL decision rule, and the append-only deviation
log are integrity safeguards as much as methodological ones: they commit us in
advance to publishing null and inconclusive outcomes rather than selectively
reporting positive findings. A negative result for the intervention study is
likewise pre-committed as a publishable finding.

\paragraph{Cost, compute, and disclosure.}
Reporting cost in dimensionless accuracy-token units first, with a dated and
sensitivity-banded monetary figure only as a secondary quantity, avoids
overstating economic claims that are sensitive to volatile provider
pricing~\cite{erol2025costofpass}. All pinned model IDs, price tables, and version
metadata are disclosed with the released artifacts so that the compute footprint
and its monetary translation are auditable.

%% ===== conclusion =====
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
Long agentic-coding sessions decay; the field has built excellent thermometers for
this and no thermostat. We pre-register a controlled harness that (i) confirms, only
as a manipulation check, that context rot transfers to agentic filler while
separating volume-driven decay from position effects; (ii) tests whether that decay
is predictable \emph{live} from cheap signals beyond what context length alone
predicts; and (iii) tests whether intervening at a detected peak beats intervening
blindly, with timing isolated from mechanism. We commit in advance to the
GO/WEAK/NULL rule and to publishing null and inconclusive outcomes. The artifacts,
including a hash-pinned context corpus and the analysis code, are released so that
the curve, the detector, and the dollar figure can be reproduced on independent
infrastructure.
